Our Mission: No One Hungry, No Food Wasted

Transforming Leftovers into sustenance, one meal at a time

The Heartbreaking Reality

20-22% of food produced in India is wasted
2 in 10 people go to bed hungry every night
12% of our population is undernourished

While restaurants, cafes, and households throw away perfectly good food, millions of our fellow citizens struggle to find their next meal. Children go to school hungry, elderly choose between medicine and food, and families face the pain of empty plates.

This isn't just about waste‚Äîit's about human dignity. It's about the single mother who skips meals so her children can eat. The student who can't concentrate in class because of hunger. The senior citizen who survives on tea and biscuits.

Why FoodShare Exists

We started FoodShare after witnessing a heartbreaking contradiction: dumpsters filled with untouched food while homeless shelters turned people away due to shortages.

We realized the problem wasn't scarcity‚Äîit was distribution. The food exists. The hungry exist. They just needed a bridge.

FoodShare is that bridge. We connect those who have extra with those who have empty. We turn surplus into survival, waste into wonder, and compassion into action.

"We don't need to produce more food. We need to share what we already have with more love."
